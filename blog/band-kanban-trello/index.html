Band + Kanban: Using of Trello

March 21, 2020 - 3 min read

In my previous post, I talked about introducing a software methodology called "Kanban" to our band's staff in order to streamline our work and see at-a-glance what needs to be prioritized. This post will discuss how we use Trello, an online Kanban organizer.

However, Trello is much more than an online Kanban board. Certainly it can be used for that, but Trello really lends itself to succinctly organizing any variety of work, adding members to help you collaborate on any project.

Kanban, at its basics, contains three columns: "To-Do", where your backlog is prioritized, "Doing" where work is currently being completed, and "Done", a record of work that has been completed, who completed it, and any additional details. If work is found to be lacking, a card can be moved back from "Done" into the backlog or "Doing." It's really that simple. Below is an example of our Trello Board, post-Coronavirus, set up to aid in the Kanban method, with personal information blurred.

If you look closely, you'll see a couple of extra columns, "Validation" and "Project Ideas". Validation is another Agile principle, where accountability for any piece of work can be checked. In our case, we verify that the portion of work has been completed satisfactorily, either confirmed by another manager or by a director. Project Ideas is more self-explanatory, a Kanban-adjacent column where board members can comment on any large project ideas.

Getting our directors and staff up and running with Trello was a breeze, as the both mobile and web application offers easy setup. The free-tier offers unlimited number of boards, as well as members to join that board.We were even able to create a "Director's Only" board, where the managers are unable to view upcoming work they would not by privy to. I had created a Google Slides for the managers and directors to quickly become onboarded. I've shared that below as well as in the Resources section below, to use at your convenience.

Trello has been a major stepping-stone towards allowing our staff to become more involved in projects, as well as bolstering communication. Trello's easy to use format lets pieces of work be created with ease, and allows to see what is being worked on at-a-glance. Directly after I had helped set up Trello with a director, they turned to me saying, "You've directly added value to this program," and that's really all the thanks I needed.
